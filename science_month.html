Science Month Reflection

Event Reflection and Documentation

Reflection Questions and Answers

1. What is the most important thing I learned from the event?

The most important thing I learned from Science Month is how science influences our daily lives and the importance of curiosity and discovery. I realized that science is not only a school subject but also a tool that helps us understand the world, solve problems, and improve our quality of life.

2. How can I apply what I learned in real-life situations?

I can apply what I learned by being more observant and analytical in solving everyday problems. Whether it’s understanding how technology works, conserving energy, or practicing proper waste management, I can use science-based knowledge to make smart and responsible decisions as a student.

3. Did I actively participate in the event? How?

Yes, I actively participated in the Science Month celebration by joining in the efforts of the WINS Corner competition and cheered on representatives of our section in the activities conducted by our school’s science club.

4. If I were to teach this topic/subject to a classmate, how would I explain it?

I would explain that Science Month is a celebration that highlights the importance of scientific learning and innovation. It encourages students to explore, experiment, and appreciate how science contributes to progress in technology, health, and the environment.

5. Why is it important to have an event (per subject)? Explain your answer.

It’s important to have subject-based events like Science Month because they make learning fun and interactive. These activities motivate students to apply classroom lessons in creative and practical ways while promoting teamwork, innovation, and curiosity.
