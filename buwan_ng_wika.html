Buwan ng Wika Reflection

Event Reflection and Documentation

Reflection Questions and Answers

1. What is the most important thing I learned from the event?

The most important thing I learned from the Buwan ng Wika celebration is the value of preserving and promoting our Filipino language and culture. The event reminded me that our language is more than just a way to communicate — it is a reflection of our identity, history, and unity as a nation.

2. How can I apply what I learned in real-life situations?

I can apply what I learned by speaking Filipino more confidently in daily conversations and by using it in writing, social media, and school activities. I will also promote our local dialects and encourage others to take pride in our heritage.

3. Did I actively participate in the event? How?

Yes, I actively participated by joining classroom activities related to Buwan ng Wika, such our declamation performance task in Filipino and wearing traditional Filipino attire every monday of the month.

4. If I were to teach this topic/subject to a classmate, how would I explain it?

I would explain that Buwan ng Wika is a celebration that honors the importance of our national language — Filipino — and reminds us to cherish our cultural roots. It encourages everyone to use our language with pride and respect, especially in schools and communities.

5. Why is it important to have an event (per subject)? Explain your answer.

It is important to have events like Buwan ng Wika because they allow us to connect what we learn in school to real-life experiences. These events make learning more meaningful and enjoyable while promoting creativity, participation, and appreciation for our culture and identity.

Event Documentation

During the Buwan ng Wika celebration, students showcased their talents through performances and cultural presentations. Everyone wore traditional Filipino clothing such as barong and filipiniana, making the event lively and colorful.
